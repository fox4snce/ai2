## 1. The 4-Operator Basis (NAND of Ideas)
- **Generate (G):** propose new candidates, variations, perturbations.
- **Compose (∘):** combine operators into higher-order structures.
- **Map (Φ):** transfer relational structure across domains (substrate shift, morphism).
- **Measure (M):** evaluate coherence, stability, generality (ℒ, S, C, H, R, CDS).

All higher operators reduce to policies over these four primitives.

## 2. Triadic Architecture (MVC as Consciousness)
- **View (External World):** environment, inputs, sensory data.
- **Controller (Internal Loop):** the 4-op engine (G,∘,Φ,M) with Boundary and Retrofit.
- **Model (Memory):** operator lattice; densified, retrofitted, stable knowledge.

Feedback closure across these three = the minimal architecture of cognition/consciousness.

## 3. Evolutionary Dynamics of Operators
- **Survival = Adaptability.**  
  Stable systems that cannot adapt collapse; adaptive systems that lack stability dissolve.  
- **Operator evolution:**  
  - Generate new patterns.  
  - Compose and Map them into candidate operators.  
  - Measure against survival pressure.  
  - Boundary prunes failed branches.  
  - Retrofit densifies survivors into memory.  
- **Transmission:** storytelling, culture, genetics, teaching, libraries → collective survival.

This is evolution reframed as operator calculus.

## 6. Boundary & Cross-Domain Stability (CDS)
Operators stabilize when:
- **Stability (S)** and **Cross-Domain Stability (CDS)** surpass thresholds.  
- Marginal gain in ℒ, S, C, R, CDS < ε for N steps.  
- **CDS metric:**  
```
CDS(p) = (M - α·E - β·X) / |D|
```
M = matches, E = extensions, X = mismatches across domains.

Boundary = Atropos: cutting the line when stability/generalization is achieved.
